Sony A7IV – Portrait · Low Light

Full Frame Mirrorless · Intermediate · Aperture Priority
Recommended ModeAperture Priority Aperturef/1.4 – f/1.8 Shutter Speed1/100s min (Auto ISO floor) ISOAuto, ceiling 12800 Focus ModeFace and Eye AF Drive ModeContinuous Low White BalanceAuto

The Key Setting

Shutter Speed Safety Net — The single most important setting in this scenario is your Auto ISO minimum shutter speed. Go into the camera menu, find Auto ISO, and set the minimum shutter speed to 1/100s. Without it the camera will drop to 1/10s or slower in dim light and every frame will be motion blurred regardless of how steady you hold the camera. Set it once and it protects every low light shot automatically.

Recommended Gear

Recommended Focal Length
50mm or 85mm prime at f/1.4 or f/1.8 — zoom lenses at f/4 or f/5.6 will force ISO so high that image quality degrades before the A7IV's sensor can compensate.
Lens Type Note
Low light is where fast primes pay for themselves. If you own an f/1.8 prime this is the scenario it was built for. A kit zoom in low light is an operator fighting the camera. A fast prime in low light is an operator working with it.
