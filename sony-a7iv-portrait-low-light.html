⚙️ Key Setting
Shutter Speed Safety Net — The single most important setting in this scenario is your Auto ISO minimum shutter speed. Go into the camera menu, find Auto ISO, and set the minimum shutter speed to 1/100s. Without it the camera will drop to 1/10s or slower in dim light and every frame will be motion blurred regardless of how steady you hold the camera. Set it once and it protects every low light shot automatically.
📷 Recommended Focal Length

50mm or 85mm prime at f1.4 or f1.8 — zoom lenses at f4 or f5.6 will force ISO so high that image quality degrades before the A7IV's sensor can compensate.

🔍 Lens Type Note

Low light is where fast primes pay for themselves. If you own an f1.8 prime this is the scenario it was built for. A kit zoom in low light is an operator fighting the camera. A fast prime in low light is an operator working with it.

“Low light is not a problem to solve. It is a constraint that reveals operator confidence. The photographer who configured the camera before entering the room shoots. Everyone else adjusts after the moment is gone.”
