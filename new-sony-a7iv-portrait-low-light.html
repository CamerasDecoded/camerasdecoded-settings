Sony A7IV Portrait Low Light Settings

Exact settings for Sony A7IV portrait photography in low light.
Recommended Mode Manual Shutter Speed 1/125s Aperture f/1.4–f/2.0 ISO Auto, cap at 6400 Focus Mode Face and Eye AF Drive Mode Single Shot White Balance Tungsten

The Key Setting

The Sony A7IV's AF Subject Shift Sensitivity is the hidden advantage here. In low light, when the camera might struggle to distinguish between the subject and the background, set AF Subject Shift Sensitivity to 1 (Locked On) — this prevents the focus from jumping to a passing element when the subject is backlit and the AF system might momentarily lose the eye against the bright sky. Enable Zebra Display set to 100+ to warn you before the rim light clips to pure white; the A7IV's dynamic range can recover highlights, but you want to avoid losing texture in the brightest areas of the subject's skin.
